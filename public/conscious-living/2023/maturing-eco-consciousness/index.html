Megatrend 1

Precarious eco consciousness represents a significant paradigm shift in the acceptance of climate change as a nation. While the country grapples with the lived realities of climate uncertainties, we are building a networked understanding not as an innocent bystander or the culprit of the crisis but as an agent of change. The focus has shifted from individual accountability to the vital role of governments, policies, and corporations in tackling the climate crisis. The trend reveals an emerging realisation that social change must be driven by collective efforts rather than isolated actions. Driven by this sentiment and fuelled by social media citizen groups are also taking matters into their own hands and organising and aligning themselves around grassroot action that takes root to form mass movements.

There is a direct relationship between social class and carbon footprint. There is an urgent need for equitable solutions. To confront these challenges head-on, campaigns and social messaging must resound louder than ever, fostering widespread awareness.
